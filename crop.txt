       <div class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
        <div class="mw-parser-output">
         <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">
          .mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}

         <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
          For other uses, see
          <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Crop_(disambiguation)" title="Crop (disambiguation)">
           Crop (disambiguation)
          </a>
          .
         </div>
         <div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">
          Plant or animal product which can be grown and harvested
         </div>
         <div class="thumb tright">
          <div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;">
           <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:New_World_Domesticated_plants.JPG">
            <img class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="1004" decoding="async" height="134" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/New_World_Domesticated_plants.JPG/280px-New_World_Domesticated_plants.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/New_World_Domesticated_plants.JPG/420px-New_World_Domesticated_plants.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/New_World_Domesticated_plants.JPG/560px-New_World_Domesticated_plants.JPG 2x" width="280"/>
           </a>
           <div class="thumbcaption">
            <div class="magnify">
             <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:New_World_Domesticated_plants.JPG" title="Enlarge">
             </a>
            </div>
            Domesticated plants
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="thumb tright">
          <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
           <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Drying_Crop_in_rural_Punjabi_home.JPG">
            <img class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Drying_Crop_in_rural_Punjabi_home.JPG/220px-Drying_Crop_in_rural_Punjabi_home.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Drying_Crop_in_rural_Punjabi_home.JPG/330px-Drying_Crop_in_rural_Punjabi_home.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Drying_Crop_in_rural_Punjabi_home.JPG/440px-Drying_Crop_in_rural_Punjabi_home.JPG 2x" width="220"/>
           </a>
           <div class="thumbcaption">
            <div class="magnify">
             <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Drying_Crop_in_rural_Punjabi_home.JPG" title="Enlarge">
             </a>
            </div>
            Crops drying in a home in
            <a href="/wiki/Punjab,_Pakistan" title="Punjab, Pakistan">
             Punjab, Pakistan
            </a>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
         <p>
          A
          <b>
           crop
          </b>
          is a
          <a href="/wiki/Plant" title="Plant">
           plant
          </a>
          that can be grown and
          <a href="/wiki/Harvest" title="Harvest">
           harvested
          </a>
          extensively for profit or
          <a href="/wiki/Subsistence_agriculture" title="Subsistence agriculture">
           subsistence
          </a>
          .
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
           <a href="#cite_note-1">
            [1]
           </a>
          </sup>
          When the plants of the same kind are cultivated at one place on a large scale, it is called a
          <b>
           crop
          </b>
          . Most crops are cultivated in
          <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">
           agriculture
          </a>
          or
          <a href="/wiki/Hydroponics" title="Hydroponics">
           hydroponics
          </a>
          . Crops may include macroscopic
          <a href="/wiki/Fungus" title="Fungus">
           fungus
          </a>
          (e.g.
          <a href="/wiki/Mushroom" title="Mushroom">
           mushrooms
          </a>
          ) and marine
          <a href="/wiki/Seaweed" title="Seaweed">
           macroalga
          </a>
          (e.g.
          <a href="/wiki/Seaweed_farming" title="Seaweed farming">
           seaweed
          </a>
          ), some of which are grown in
          <a href="/wiki/Aquaculture" title="Aquaculture">
           aquaculture
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Most crops are
          <a href="/wiki/Harvest" title="Harvest">
           harvested
          </a>
          as
          <a href="/wiki/Food" title="Food">
           food
          </a>
          for humans or
          <a href="/wiki/Fodder" title="Fodder">
           fodder
          </a>
          for
          <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">
           livestock
          </a>
          . Some crops are gathered from the wild often in a form of
          <a href="/wiki/Intensive_gathering" title="Intensive gathering">
           intensive gathering
          </a>
          (e.g.
          <a href="/wiki/Ginseng" title="Ginseng">
           ginseng
          </a>
          ,
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yohimbe" title="Yohimbe">
           yohimbe
          </a>
          , and
          <a href="/wiki/Eucommia" title="Eucommia">
           eucommia
          </a>
          ).
         </p>
         <p>
          Important non-food crops include horticulture, floriculture and industrial crops.
          <a href="/wiki/Horticulture" title="Horticulture">
           Horticulture
          </a>
          crops include plants used for other crops (e.g.
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fruit_trees" title="Fruit trees">
           fruit trees
          </a>
          ).
          <a href="/wiki/Floriculture" title="Floriculture">
           Floriculture
          </a>
          crops include bedding plants, houseplants, flowering garden and pot plants, cut cultivated greens, and
          <a href="/wiki/Cut_flowers" title="Cut flowers">
           cut flowers
          </a>
          .
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_crop" title="Industrial crop">
           Industrial crops
          </a>
          are produced for
          <a href="/wiki/Clothing" title="Clothing">
           clothing
          </a>
          (
          <a href="/wiki/Fiber_crop" title="Fiber crop">
           fiber crops
          </a>
          e.g.
          <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">
           cotton
          </a>
          ),
          <a href="/wiki/Biofuel" title="Biofuel">
           biofuel
          </a>
          (
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Energy_crops" title="Energy crops">
           energy crops
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="/wiki/Algae_fuel" title="Algae fuel">
           algae fuel
          </a>
          ), or
          <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">
           medicine
          </a>
          (
          <a href="/wiki/Medicinal_plants" title="Medicinal plants">
           medicinal plants
          </a>
          ).
         </p>
         <meta property="mw:PageProp/toc"/>
         <h2>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="Important_food_crops">
           Important food crops
          </span>
          <span class="mw-editsection">
           <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
            [
           </span>
           <a href="/w/index.php?title=Crop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Important food crops">
            edit
           </a>
           <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
            ]
           </span>
          </span>
         </h2>
         <div class="thumb tright">
          <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
           <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:World_production_of_crops_by_commodity_group.svg">
            <img class="thumbimage" data-file-height="347" data-file-width="512" decoding="async" height="149" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/World_production_of_crops_by_commodity_group.svg/220px-World_production_of_crops_by_commodity_group.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/World_production_of_crops_by_commodity_group.svg/330px-World_production_of_crops_by_commodity_group.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/World_production_of_crops_by_commodity_group.svg/440px-World_production_of_crops_by_commodity_group.svg.png 2x" width="220"/>
           </a>
           <div class="thumbcaption">
            <div class="magnify">
             <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:World_production_of_crops_by_commodity_group.svg" title="Enlarge">
             </a>
            </div>
            World production of crops by commodity group
            <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
             <a href="#cite_note-2">
              [2]
             </a>
            </sup>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
         <p>
          The importance of a crop varies greatly by region. Globally, the following crops contribute most to human food supply (values of kcal/person/day for 2013 given in parentheses):
          <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">
           rice
          </a>
          (541 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Wheat" title="Wheat">
           wheat
          </a>
          (527 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">
           sugarcane
          </a>
          and other sugar crops (200 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Maize" title="Maize">
           maize
          </a>
          (corn) (147 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Soybean_oil" title="Soybean oil">
           soybean oil
          </a>
          (82 kcal), other
          <a href="/wiki/Vegetable" title="Vegetable">
           vegetables
          </a>
          (74 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Potato" title="Potato">
           potatoes
          </a>
          (64 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Palm_oil" title="Palm oil">
           palm oil
          </a>
          (52 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Cassava" title="Cassava">
           cassava
          </a>
          (37 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Legume" title="Legume">
           legume
          </a>
          pulses (37 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Sunflower_seed" title="Sunflower seed">
           sunflower seed
          </a>
          oil (35 kcal),
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rape_oil" title="Rape oil">
           rape
          </a>
          and
          <a href="/wiki/Mustard_oil" title="Mustard oil">
           mustard oil
          </a>
          (34 kcal), other
          <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">
           fruits
          </a>
          , (31 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Sorghum" title="Sorghum">
           sorghum
          </a>
          (28 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Millet" title="Millet">
           millet
          </a>
          (27 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Peanut" title="Peanut">
           groundnuts
          </a>
          (25 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Bean" title="Bean">
           beans
          </a>
          (23 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Sweet_potato" title="Sweet potato">
           sweet potatoes
          </a>
          (22 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Banana" title="Banana">
           bananas
          </a>
          (21 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Nut_(fruit)" title="Nut (fruit)">
           various nuts
          </a>
          (16 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Soybean" title="Soybean">
           soybeans
          </a>
          (14 kcal),
          <a href="/wiki/Cottonseed_oil" title="Cottonseed oil">
           cottonseed oil
          </a>
          (13 kcal),
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Groundnut_oil" title="Groundnut oil">
           groundnut oil
          </a>
          (13 kcal),

         <h2>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="Methods_of_cropping_and_popular_crops_in_the_U.S.">
           Methods of cropping and popular crops in the U.S.
          </span>
          <span class="mw-editsection">
           <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
            [
           </span>
           <a href="/w/index.php?title=Crop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Methods of cropping and popular crops in the U.S.">
            edit
           </a>
           <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
            ]
           </span>
          </span>
         </h2>
         <p>
          There are various methods of cropping that are used in the agricultural industry,
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_6-0">
           <a href="#cite_note-:0-6">
            [6]
           </a>
          </sup>
          such as mono cropping, crop rotation, sequential cropping, and mixed intercropping.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
           <a href="#cite_note-7">
            [7]
           </a>
          </sup>
          Each method of cropping have their purposes and possibly disadvantages to them as well.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
           <a href="#cite_note-8">
            [8]
           </a>
          </sup>
          <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Himanshu_Arora&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Himanshu Arora (page does not exist)">
           Himanshu Arora
          </a>
          defines mono cropping as where a field only grows one specific crop year round.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_6-1">
           <a href="#cite_note-:0-6">
            [6]
           </a>
          </sup>
          Mono Cropping has its disadvantages, according to Himanshu Arora, such as the risk of the soil losing its fertility.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
           <a href="#cite_note-9">
            [9]
           </a>
          </sup>
          Following mono cropping, another method of cropping is relay cropping. According to the National Library of Medicine, relay cropping may solve a number of conflicts such as inefficient use of available resources, controversies in sowing time, fertilizer application, and soil degradation.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
           <a href="#cite_note-10">
            [10]
           </a>
          </sup>
          The result coming from the use of relay cropping is higher crop output.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
           <a href="#cite_note-11">
            [11]
           </a>
          </sup>
          In the United States, corn is the largest crop produced, and soybean follows in at second, according to the government of Alberta.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
           <a href="#cite_note-12">
            [12]
           </a>
          </sup>
          Referring to a map given by the Government of Alberta, the most popular region to grow these popular crops are in the inner states of the U.S., it is where the crops are most successful in output.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
           <a href="#cite_note-13">
            [13]
           </a>
          </sup>
         </p>
